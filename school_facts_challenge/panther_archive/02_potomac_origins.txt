THE POTOMAC SCHOOL: FROM DUPONT CIRCLE TO McLEAN

The Potomac School was founded in 1904 by Edith Draper Blair, Hetty Fairfax Harrison, and Ellen Warder Thoron. Its first 48 students attended classes in a rented building near Dupont Circle in Washington, D.C.

Decades later, the school sought more room and purchased 55 acres in McLean. Potomac's new campus opened in 1951 with an architectural emphasis on natural light and easy access to the outdoors. The campus continued to expand over time, while the school added an Upper School in the late 1980s. Today, Potomac describes itself as a connected K-12 community on one campus.

The school's mission emphasizes lives of purpose, achievement, and generosity of spirit. Its athletic teams are known as the Panthers.
