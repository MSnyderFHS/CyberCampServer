FRIDAY ASSEMBLIES AT POTOMAC

Friday assemblies have roots reaching back to The Potomac School's founding in 1904. In the Lower School, students gather to sing, practice public speaking, present what they have learned, and develop the habits of attentive audience members. Families may attend these weekly gatherings.

The tradition shows how a repeated school event can serve several purposes at once. An assembly builds community, but it can also provide a stage for music, speaking, and academic demonstrations. Potomac's Lower School traditions also include a Halloween assembly and a third-grade science fair.

Because Potomac is a K-12 school on one campus, schoolwide traditions can help students see themselves as members of a larger community that extends beyond their own grade or division.
