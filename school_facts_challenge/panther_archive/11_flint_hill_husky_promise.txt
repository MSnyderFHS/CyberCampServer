THE HUSKY PROMISE AND TEAMWORK

Flint Hill uses the Husky as more than an athletic symbol. Lower School students learn a Husky Promise centered on doing their best, respecting themselves and others, acting responsibly and honestly, and showing compassion.

The school explains the promise with a sled-team metaphor. A group of huskies succeeds when each member contributes and the team crosses the finish line together. This links the mascot to cooperation throughout school life.

The Husky identity dates to a student vote in the early 1990s. Before that change, Flint Hill teams were known as the Falcons. The later mascot choice became part of the school's traditions and vocabulary, including the name Husky used for students and the costumed mascot Klondike.
