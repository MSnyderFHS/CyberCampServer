LEARNING OUTDOORS AT POTOMAC

The Potomac School's McLean campus contains wooded trails, streams, two named ponds, gardens, fields, and outdoor classrooms. The school presents these places as an extension of its indoor classrooms and as resources for environmental education.

Five outdoor classrooms appear in the school's campus description. One wooded location includes a deck and fire pit that can be used during winter. The published campus map also identifies Pimmit Run, a butterfly garden, the Morton Fire Deck, the Tundra green, and several athletic fields.

Outdoor access influenced Potomac's move to McLean. When the campus opened in 1951, the design emphasized natural light and convenient access to the outdoors. That idea remains visible in the school's modern description of a campus where many classrooms open toward the surrounding landscape.
