FLINT HILL: A SHORT ORIGIN NOTE

Flint Hill School traces its history to 1956, when Flint Hill Preparatory was located on Chain Bridge Road in Fairfax, Virginia. The school later went through a major reorganization. In 1989, John Tilghman "Til" Hazel led the acquisition and reorganization of Flint Hill Preparatory. The following year, the school opened as Flint Hill School for students in kindergarten through grade 12 on the campus now known as Hazel Lower School.

The school's identity also changed. Flint Hill teams were once called the Falcons. After a student vote in the early 1990s, the school adopted the Huskies as its mascot. That choice developed into a larger school theme: a sled team succeeds when everyone works together.
