TWO INDEPENDENT SCHOOLS IN NORTHERN VIRGINIA

Flint Hill School and The Potomac School are both independent schools in Fairfax County, Virginia, but their campuses have different layouts. Flint Hill serves grades JK-12 in Oakton and reports 50 acres across two campuses. Its Upper School campus is on Jermantown Road.

Potomac serves grades K-12 on a single 90-acre campus in McLean. Potomac's grounds include woods, ponds, streams, gardens, athletic fields, and outdoor classrooms. The school notes that its location is only a few miles from Washington, D.C.

Both schools combine academics with arts, athletics, clubs, and community activities. Their mascots make the contrast easy to remember: Flint Hill students are Huskies, while Potomac students are Panthers.
