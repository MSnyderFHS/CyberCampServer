POTOMAC WOODLAND LOG

The Potomac School's 90-acre campus is more than a collection of buildings. Its woods, streams, ponds, fields, and gardens are also used as learning spaces. Potomac reports that five outdoor classrooms give teachers options for lessons outside. A deck with a fire pit sits in the woods, and the school notes that students may see whitetail deer and other native wildlife.

Campus trails have a long history. Potomac says that forests, streams, ponds, and fields have been part of school traditions since trails were established in the 1950s. The campus map includes Upper Pond, Lower Pond, Pimmit Run, outdoor classrooms, a butterfly garden, and the Morton Fire Deck.

Environmental stewardship is therefore connected to the physical layout of the school rather than being limited to a single classroom.

Source: https://www.potomacschool.org/about-potomac/our-campus
