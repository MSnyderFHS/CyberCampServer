POTOMAC CAMPUS INVENTORY

The Potomac School reports more than one hundred classrooms on its McLean campus. It also lists three libraries, science and computer labs, art and photography studios, two dining rooms, common spaces, and study areas.

The 90-acre grounds add many outdoor features. Students have access to woods, ponds, streams, gardens, playgrounds, athletic fields, a track, tennis courts, an outdoor pool, and several outdoor classrooms. The school says that nearly every classroom has a door leading outside, supporting movement between indoor and outdoor learning.

One unusual item on Potomac's published campus map is a llama statue. The same map identifies the Upper School East and Tundra buildings, the Quad and Upper School Bridge, two ponds, Pimmit Run, and several athletic areas.

Source: https://www.potomacschool.org/about-potomac/our-campus
