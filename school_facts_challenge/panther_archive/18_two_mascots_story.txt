THE HUSKY AND THE PANTHER

A husky is a working dog associated with sled teams and cold climates. A panther is a name commonly used for a large, powerful cat. The two animals make very different school symbols.

At Flint Hill, the Husky represents teamwork. The school uses the image of every member pulling the sled so that the whole group reaches the finish line. Students are called Huskies, and the costumed mascot is known as Klondike.

At The Potomac School, teams compete as the Panthers. Potomac's athletics pages, school news, and records use the Panther identity across many sports.

When the two schools compete, the matchup becomes Huskies versus Panthers. The contrast between a cooperative sled dog and a stealthy wild cat gives a friendly school rivalry plenty of storytelling possibilities.
