TRADITIONS, IDENTITY, AND COMMUNITY

School traditions often turn ordinary events into shared memories. At Potomac, Friday assemblies date back to the school's early years. Students sing, speak, present work, and practice being attentive audience members. A K-12 campus gives younger and older students many chances to see themselves as members of one connected institution.

At Flint Hill, the Husky identity is tied to the idea that every member helps pull the sled. The mascot was selected by students in the early 1990s, replacing the earlier Falcon identity. The Husky Promise introduces values such as responsibility, honesty, respect, compassion, and doing one's best.

The schools use different traditions, but both connect individual effort with participation in a larger community. Their Panther and Husky mascots give those communities memorable symbols, especially when the schools meet in athletics.
