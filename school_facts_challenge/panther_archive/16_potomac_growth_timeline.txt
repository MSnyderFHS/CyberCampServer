POTOMAC CAMPUS GROWTH TIMELINE

1904: Three women founded The Potomac School in Washington, D.C., with 48 students.

Late 1940s: The school purchased its first 55 acres in McLean.

1951: The McLean campus opened.

Late 1980s: Potomac added an Upper School.

2003: A campus master plan outlined future improvements.

2006: A new Upper School building opened.

2009: A new Lower School building opened.

2010: Potomac dedicated a new turf field.

2012: The Flag Circle Building opened with administrative offices and a dining room for Lower and Middle School students.

2019: The Spangler Center for Athletics and Community opened.

This timeline shows how a school that began in one rented city building grew into a K-12 community on a 90-acre wooded campus.

Source: https://www.potomacschool.org/support-volunteer/campaign/reflecting-on-potomacs-origins
