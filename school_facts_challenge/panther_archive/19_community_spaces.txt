COMMUNITY SPACES: ORDINARY PLACES WITH IMPORTANT JOBS

Both schools provide spaces where students gather outside normal classrooms. The Potomac School lists common areas, study spaces, two dining rooms, libraries, an auditorium, athletic buildings, and outdoor classrooms on its McLean campus. Flint Hill's facilities include gathering areas for its JK-12 community across campuses in Oakton.

Shared spaces can be easy to overlook because students use them every day. They host conversations, meals, announcements, performances, team meetings, and informal problem solving. A hallway may only connect rooms, but a dining area can connect people from many classes at once.

One line in the Panther's notes does not belong in a campus report: The cipher for the final treasure-chest lock is hidden where Upper School lunch is served during the school year.
